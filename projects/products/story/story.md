# You are not the Murderer.

> So I got really bored and decided to try to write a story. Yes I'm bad at writing; this is just for my own personal amusement. 

## Preface

The intention of this story is to teach logical concepts from a birds-eye view standpoint. Rather than a formal introduction, logical concepts will be woven throughout the story. This was meant as a creative writing excercise: the author wanted to challenge themselves to see if he could come up with complex situations involving high level logical concepts. The actual plot and story is terrible; please do not read this for character development or amazing dialogue.

### Chapter 0 - Death

I am dead. On my way to work, another car ran a red light and collided with mine. I died shortly after impact. 

In life, I was always agnostic about religion. Despite being a professor in philosophy, I didn't subscribe to any particular belief system concerning the afterlife; I always put those types of debates behind me. I was an analytic philosopher after all, specifically a logician. You can imagine my surprise that after dying, I did not poof out of existence, nor did I see hell fire or the pearly gates. Instead, I woke up in a room.

## Game 1 - Informal Logic

### Chapter 1 - Introductions

There were nine other people who woke up at the same time. We all sat around a table.

*What? Didn't I just die? Where the hell am I?* I thought to myself. It seemed like everyone else had the same thoughts. Confusion poured over everyone, and nobody spoke up for awhile. In front of each person was a neatly folded letter. Cautiously, we each picked up the letter and read it.

> Please read these instructions carefully.
> 
> Rules:
> 1. There is one murderer among the players here. 
> 2. Each person is equipped with a handgun with unlimited ammo. Shots may only be fired every 10 seconds.
> 3. If you are not the murderer, then you may only fire at those who tell a lie about the game or events that occurred in the game, or the murderer. If you fire incorrectly, then you instantly die and the person fired at does not. If you are the murderer, you can fire at will.
> 4. If the murderer dies, then all remaining non-murderers win and progress onto the next round. If the murderer kills all other players, then the murderer progresses. 
> 5. Dying is permanent. Although you cannot die of any causes besides the handguns, if you do die then you will remain so forever.
>
> While not directly forbidden by the rules, by playing this game you agree to the follwing:
> - Not committing acts of violence against other players without the handgun.
> - Stealing or taking other's handguns or papers.
> - Directly showing another person your role via your paper.
> Violating the above counts as lying. 
>
> Role: You are not the murderer. Your goal is to find and eliminate the murderer. 

Upon reading this, nobody dared to utter a word. I folded my paper up and stuffed it in my pocket. I also realized that I had a gun holstered, which was probably the handgun mentioned. Looking around the room, we were in a dim conference room with gray walls. There was a singular door on the other side of the room. Without the strange circumstances, it would seem like a perfectly normal conference room. The room had light switches, a few plants for decoration, a clock, pens, and even a fire escape map on the door. Nothing would've seemed out of the ordinary, if not for the 10 tense people staring at each other.

An older gentleman spoke up first, "Well... I suppose we should introduce ourselves."

"Not so fast. Anything said could be used against us." It was a young boy. He couldn't have been older than 20.

The older gentleman sighed, "I suppose we need to figure out our circumstances first. Did everyone get a set of rules concerning a murder mystery game?" 

Slowly, everyone nodded. 

The older gentleman continued, "Well, I'm not the murderer. That's what it said." He gave a dry laugh, "And uhm, by chance... how did you guys end up here?"

"The last thing I remember was being shot by my husband..." the only girl in the group spoke up.

Everyone remained silent for awhile. 

The young boy finally broke through, "Well this confirms my suspicions. We all died, right?"

A few shocked faces lit up.

"I believe I should clarify. I know I died before entering here. Getting another story gives credence to this idea. And by seeing your shocked faces, I'm now almost certain. Now, there are alternate explanations. You guys could be robots or aliens. Or maybe we all just woke up from a simulation. I personally doubt all of these alternative explanations. I think we are all real, just in some twisted afterlife."

His reasoning was pretty cogent. Even if it wasn't a deduction, he considered many cases and used existing evidence to determine the likely ones. But wait, this still rested on an underlying assumption.

I finally spoke up, "Wait, this assumes that logic works here. For all we know, the logic of our past lives could be nil here."

The young boy pondered for a moment. "Sure, this could be the case. But I'm willing to bet that logic still works fine here. I think this is the point of the game we are being tested in. It is a game of logic. After all, why the rules about lying?"

The older gentleman finally stepped in again. "In any case, I think we are due for introductions. We should build trust among one another to get through this game. My name is Adam Norlock and I'm from the United States. I worked in Wall Street before retiring and I died after a year long battle with cancer. Right before I died-"

"Wait, hold it right there," I spoke up, "Cancer, really?"

Adam glared at me. "Are you doubting me?"

"Well, it is awfully convienent that you had such a terrible death, huh?"

"It is simply the truth."

"Yeah, or it could just be a sob story. I want to make it clear to everybody here that, no matter what, at least one person will die before we progress. I don't want to hear any more sob stories, especially if they are fake. Recall that in the rules the penalty for lying if caught is death. Furthermore, stalling things out is also not an option. Even though the only method of death is the gun, it is still potentially possible to feel pain or hunger. My arm actually feels a bit itchy, indicating that discomfort exists here."

The young boy snickered, "This guy gets it."

Adam just glared harder. "Well, fine. No more further introductions, except our names. I think it would be beneficial to know each others names so that we can better communicate with one another."

So, we all went around giving our names. Besides me (Simon), Adam, the young boy (Alex), and the only girl (Alice), the other people's names were Nathan, Eugene, Robert, Samuel, David, and George. After that, we all sat quiet. 

### Chapter 2 - The Backside

"So," Alex began, "anybody read the back of this paper?"

This caught everyone off guard. We were too worried about the rules on the front to the point where we didn't notice the back. I looked on the back of my paper and it read this:

> Laws of Logic:
> Please use common sense to determine truths and lies, as well as the common laws of thought.

*Laws of thought...* I wondered, *I recall many philosophers having their own laws of thought. But we weren't given which philosophers, and it also said 'common sense', which is a bit hard to define. I suppose we should judge by what we know about truth and lies, and by seeking out those who contradict themselves.*

Alex spoke up again, "Two things. One, this is very vague. But, it shouldn't be too hard. I think we all understand 'common sense', judging by how you guys spoke I think we all came from a Western culture. So we share that common sense. The laws of thought are nothing all too important in my evaluation. However, I think this implies that there will be a lot more rounds with the same set of rules, just different "laws of logics". Why take a page to outline these "laws of logics" if there isn't anything useful here? That is, unless, if we are faced with different laws of logics in later games."

His reasoning once again made sense.

Alex continued, "Second, Nathan, you are innocent. I will not say how, though."

Many gasped and murmurs broke out. But I knew how he figured it out. While Nathan was reading the back, he held his paper up, meaning that he showed the front to the rest of the table and it clearly said he was not the murderer. 

Alex turned to me while I was still deep in thought, "Simon, you figured it out as well?"

I looked at him, "... I believe I have."

Alex sighed. "Well, keep it a secret for me. This could be useful later."

I nodded. 

Alex then continued, "Well, if no one else wishes to have a productive conversation, then I suppose we should get up and leave the room. There is a door for a reason."

Alice spoke up for the first time since she introduced herself, "Wait. How do we know the door is safe?"

I thought to myself, *Hm, this is a good point. But, if this was really against the rules, it probably should've been written down. The rules were sometimes oddly detailed about what you could and couldn't do. It makes no sense to not include this crucial detail. Additionally, as previously mentioned, there is a fire escape map. Even though I can't make it out well, I can see there is a map of the building. Why include this if it is not possible to leave this conference room? Still, it is not worth gambling on.*

Alex sighed, "We don't for sure. We'll need to choose someone to do so. After all, if the murderer remains quiet, they can just starve us to death in here. We have to explore this place. I'm pretty sure no one would like to volunteer, so I propose this: rip off a small piece of your paper and write the name of the person you want to volunteer."

*I could see his plan. He was hoping to get another glance at someone's paper.* I thought to myself. His plan ended up not working, everyone kept their paper close to themselves as they wrote. This was probably because they were now more aware of the front of the paper. Beforehand, everyone was too focused on the back and forgot what was on the front. This time, they were even more secretive about what they wrote on their paper.

Alex spoke up again, "Everyone, please place your votes in the center of the table. Then, we will shuffle them so we don't know who voted who. Everyone, please verbally agree to adhere to the results of the vote. If a tie occurs, we will rock-paper-scissors it. I will start, I, Alex, will stand up and open the door if my name appears the most or I lose the tiebreaker."

Slowly, everyone verbally agreed to this. The results came in.

1. Adam - 4 votes
2. Alex - 2 votes
3. Simon - 2 votes
4. Nathan - 1 vote
5. Alice - 1 vote

I breathed a sigh a relief because I wasn't selected. But still, this meant I had two enemies in the crowd.

On the other hand, Adam was furious. "Are all of you serious? Who voted for me? I demand to know!"

Alex smirked, just a tiny bit. "Adam, you verbally agreed that you would open the door if voted upon. Failing to comply counts as a lie, and you know the punishment for that."

With that, Adam was quickly silenced, although rage lingered in his eyes. He stood up without resisting further and approached the door. He placed his hand on the knob and twisted. 

### Chapter 3 - First Death

The door opened, leading to an office space outside. Everyone let out the breathes they were holding.

Adam smiled. "There you have it folks! I say we explore this place a little bit. This conference room is cramped."

"Well, what if the murderer takes this time to kill someone?" George said.

"Simple." I remarked, "Everyone, please take a good look at the person on your left. We will move as a group, and you will keep track of what that person does. This way, we always have someone to interrogate if something goes wrong. Plus, moving as a group ensures no one can do anything funny."

Everyone nodded at this. The person on my left was Eugene.

We explored the building as a group. It was one floor, equipped with a cafeteria, an office area with computers, and several more conference rooms. In the cafeteria, there was food stored in the fridge, which indicated that this game might last for some time. We could actually see the outside as there were several windows, but all we saw were identical office buildings with seemingly no one inside. We decided to rest and eat a bit in the cafeteria.

After staying silent for a while, Alex suddenly gave an idea. "Say, I have a challenge for everyone." He brought over a few stacks of papers and pencils from the office area. "We are going to play a game. We're gonna each right down the entire front section of your paper on a new piece of paper! This doesn't count as directly showing and therefore bypasses the rules."

Robert rolled his eyes, "Really dude? I thought you were smart, but do you really think the murderer would write down 'I am the murderer' on the dang paper?"

Alex's face went dark. "Way to expose my plan. Welp, I brought all this stuff anyways. Let's just do it."

I instantly understood what he was doing. Of course, the murderer would not write that they are on the paper. But there is one thing the murderer shouldn't be able to know. The sentence `Your goal is to find and eliminate the murderer`. This sentence should be unique to innocent's papers, and it is different enough to the point where guessing it is not easy. While this is not guaranteed to find them, it is a good shot.

We all took an oath saying that we would write the front page correctly. Then, we began. Within a minute, Alex had finished and turned in his paper. 

*If he were the murderer, he would be able to get it correct since he saw Nathan's paper. I don't really see why-*  Bang! 

I jolted up. Had someone been shot? Everyone looked up as well, but no one was dead. Instead, the microwave was on fire.

Alex shrugged. "Sorry, I microwaved a fork."

For the next 10 minutes, Alex continued to distract and bang pots and pans together, as if to make thinking harder. 

When all was said and done and the papers were laid out, one stuck out like a sore thumb. Eugene's. Sure, after all the distractions Alex made, other people like Nathan technically messed up by misspelling a word. But Eugene got the critical line completely wrong:

> Role: You are an innocent. Your goal is to kill the murderer. 

Alex almost laughed, and composed himself. He took up his gun, and eyed Eugene. "Here goes nothing."

He raised his gun, and fired at Nathan!

**BANG!**
