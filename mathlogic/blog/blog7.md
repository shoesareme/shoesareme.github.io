### Terryology

Most of you should know who Terrence Howard is. For those who don't, he is an actor that has some 'interesting' theories. We will focus on $$1 \times 1 = 2$$.

#### The Argument

According to Howard, this is the proof that $$1 \times 1 = 2$$. You can search up his original argument online, I modified the wording to make it readable.

**Theorem (Howard).** $$1 \times 1 = 2.$$

_Proof._ Assume $$1 \times 1 = 1$$. Add $$1$$ to both sides. We recieve $$1 + (1 \times 1) = 2$$. Using the associative and communative laws, we arrive at the conclusion $$3 = 2$$, contradiction. An equation must be balanced. Therefore $$1 \times 1 = 2$$. $$\Box$$

I hope I got that right. He also makes another argument where he subtracts $$1$$ from both sides.

#### Mathematical Analysis

Did you catch his mistake? It is honestly really funny. The only reason why he got $$3 = 2$$ is because he assumed $$1 \times 1 = 2$$! Indeed, $$1 + 2 = 3 \neq 2$$. I don't think the assiative and communative laws play a factor here (at least the ones he defines). It also seems that he thinks multiplication $$a \times b$$ means what we would write as $$a \times (b + 1)$$ but honestly I can't even tell.

#### Unreal Analysis

Now, I will try to use his math to derive stuff. Obviously, two corollaries follow that Howard derived. That is,

**Corollary.** $$\frac{2}{1}=1$$.

**Corollary.** $$\sqrt{2}=1$$.

From what I have gathered, it seems that Howard believes in conservation a bit too much. For example, $$1 \times 1 = 2$$ or else it "violates conservation of energy". There is no such thing as $$0$$ either.

Originally, I was about to propose a theorem where the sum of everything remains constant and all equations are the same or whatever to try to match his level of crazy, when I found myself doomscrolling through his book. Then, I encountered something that made me realize that I had no chance. Dear readers, I present to you:

**Theorem (Howard).** $$1^{3} = \|\pi\|$$

I give up.
